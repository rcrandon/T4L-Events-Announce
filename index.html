Color of Wellness Conference

Date & Time

February 29, 2024 | 11am ET - 3pm ET | UMD Riggs Alumni Center, 7801 Alumni Dr., College Park, MD

Theme

Conversations to Promote Health, Wellness and Equity | Cancer Awareness

Moderator

Adrianna Hopkins

  • 1st Segment: Black Genes Matter - Real Talk About Cancer (30 mins)
  • 2nd Segment: When We Trial and For the Love of My Gurls (30 min)
  • 3rd Segment: SafeHER Black Beauty (30 mins)
  • 4th Segment: B-FRESH (Food Restores, Energizes and Sustains Health) (30 mins)
  • 5th Segment: Ask the Doctor (30 mins)

Agenda

11:06 - 11:40

Black Genes Matter - Real Talk About Cancer

Speakers: Angela Brade, Kemith Calvin, Laura Crandon, Moniesha Jackson Shorter

Is there a genetic link to health conditions? Panelists share personal stories about their health journeys. Learn about the state of Black health and be inspired to take action to eliminate the generational impact of cancer.

11:40 - 12:10

When We Trial and For the Love of My Gurls

Speakers: Ricki Fairley, Hayley Brown

Discuss myths and facts about clinical trials. Learn why the lack of diversity in clinical trials impacts our quality of life and how we can change that.

12:46 - 1:16

SafeHER Black Beauty

Speaker: Tiah Tomlin Harris

Prioritize your health and learn how to take actionable steps to reduce your risk for breast cancer by identifying and eliminating toxins from your everyday environment and personal care routines.

1:26 - 1:56

B-FRESH (Food Restores, Energizes and Sustains Health)

Speaker: Sharon Hawks

Food and fitness as medicine will be the focus of this segment.

1:56 - 2:27

Ask the Doctor

Speakers: Dr. Jenni Sheng, Dr. Michelle Townsend-Day, Dr. Sheila Rajagopal, Dr. Cathy Handy Marshall

It’s your turn to ask leading medical and scientific experts. Learn about the latest medical advances and what is precision medicine.

2:27 - 2:57

Mindfulness and Mental Health

Speakers: Denise Patterson McKenney, Rozmyn K. Miller

The mind/body connection…how are they intrinsically linked? This workshop will explore mind/body awareness and how to holistically reduce the psychological effects of stress, illness, and some traumatic events.

Dr. Jenni Sheng

Assistant Professor of Oncology at the Johns Hopkins Sidney Kimmel Comprehensive Cancer Center

Dr. Jennifer Y. Sheng is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Oncology at the Sidney Kimmel Comprehensive Cancer Center (SKCCC) at the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine (JHUSOM). As a breast medical oncologist, she focuses on comprehensive survivorship care with emphasis on symptom management, psychosocial aspects of care, and health promotion (diet, exercise and interpersonal relationships). Her prior research includes capturing adverse symptoms of endocrine therapy, identifying scalable tools for early diagnosis of potentially debilitating side effects, understanding health patterns and comorbidities in breast cancer survivors, and exploring strategies to address weight management. Dr. Sheng’s current research focuses on personalizing and adapting weight loss interventions, digitalizing provider and patient education, and enhancing quality of life during and after cancer. To do so, she collaborates with a multidisciplinary team conducting metabolic reprogramming interventions and cardiometabolic strategies at JHUSOM. She is also planning studies to understand and improve body composition in breast cancer survivors, pinpoint clinical and CT derived biomarkers that can predict response to lifestyle interventions, and clarify the underlying obesity gene signatures that may impact cancer outcomes and survivorship. Before joining the Johns Hopkins faculty in July 2019, Dr. Sheng completed her fellowship in medical oncology at the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine. She received her MD at UMDNJ-New Jersey Medical School in 2013 and completed her undergraduate studies in Biology and Psychology at Rutgers University in 2010. She completed her Internal Medicine training at Temple University Hospitali n 2016, and fellowship training in Medical Oncology at Sidney Kimmel Comprehensive Cancer Center (SKCCC) at Johns Hopkins in 2019.

Tiah Tomlin-Harris

CEO & Founder of My Style Matters

Following a diagnosis of an aggressive form of breast cancer, or Can’t-cer as she calls it, Tiah Tomlin-Harris vowed to God that if she made it through breast cancer, she would help as many women as she could fight and survive breast cancer, particularly Black women, who are disproportionately impacted by this disease. Tiah believed that no sister should walk this journey alone and founded the My Breast Years Ahead Sisterhood, a sisterhood of women survivors. She also founded My Style Matters, Inc., a 501c3 non-profit organization that focuses on improving health outcomes and overall well-being by providing support, resources, education as well as healthy lifestyle experiences to cancer survivors and their loved ones. In 2019, upon obtaining news of a recurrence, Tiah opted to fight cancer a second time with food and faith. She adopted a whole food plant-based diet, removed products containing toxins or other dangerous chemicals from her home, and added other complementary practices to her daily routine. Tiah believes that making healthy lifestyle changes can help improve one’s quality of life and help with better life outcomes. She is now helping others on their quest for optimal health as a licensed Food for Life Instructor and is educating individuals on ways to eliminate harmful exposures. Tiah is an activist, an advocate, a Cancer Coach, a Breast Health Educator, a consultant, and a Project Lead graduate of the National Breast Cancer Coalition (NBCC). She developed Breast Health Matters, an empowerment and educational platform for teen girls and women, and serves as the Program Manager for the Knowledge Is Power symposium focusing on Black women diagnosed with breast cancer. Tiah has advocated for survivors on Capitol Hill; is a Community Advisory Board member with the Bench to Community Initiative and the Non-toxic Black Beauty Project; serves as a board member and advisory board member for several pharmaceutical companies and organizations; published a research paper on Clinical Trials in Metastatic Breast Cancer; has spoken on numerous talk shows and panels to include Tavis Smiley talk radio, American Society for Clinical Oncology (ASCO), American Association for Cancer Research (AACR) and the Congressional Briefing with Breast Cancer Prevention Partners (BCPP). Through her life's work, she has received recognition and awards, including President Bush’s – Point of Lights Award and the Georgia Center for Oncology Research and Education (GA CORE) Phillip Street Community Service Award. Tiah is bringing H.O.P.E. to our community by educating, motivating, and activating people to move from awareness to taking action.

Dr. Padma (Sheila) Rajagopal
Physician-Scientist Early Investigator
Cancer Data Science Laboratory and Women’s Malignancies Branch
National Cancer Institute

Dr. Padma (Sheila) Rajagopal received her M.D. at Columbia University’s College of Physicians and Surgeons, during which she also received an M.P.H. in quantitative methods with a focus on genetic epidemiology at the Harvard School of Public Health. She completed her internal medicine residency at the University of Pittsburgh and a fellowship in hematology/oncology and additional fellowship year focused on cancer genetics at the University of Chicago, where she received an M.Sc. in biomedical informatics.

She has been a physician-scientist early investigator at the National Cancer Institute since 2021. Her clinical practice focuses on patients with both metastatic breast cancers (primarily triple-negative breast cancer), as well as hereditary cancer syndromes. Her laboratory investigates how genetic data (both what we are born with and from the tumor in patients affected by cancer) can be used to improve understanding of patient outcomes and predicted response to treatment.

Tiah Tomlin-Harris

Following a diagnosis of an aggressive form of breast cancer, or Can’t-cer as she calls it, Tiah Tomlin-Harris vowed to God that if she made it through breast cancer, she would help as many women as she could fight and survive breast cancer, particularly Black women, who are disproportionately impacted by this disease.

Tiah believed that no sister should walk this journey alone and founded the My Breast Years Ahead Sisterhood, a sisterhood of women survivors. She also founded My Style Matters, Inc., a 501c3 non-profit organization that focuses on improving health outcomes and overall well-being by providing support, resources, education as well as healthy lifestyle experiences to cancer survivors and their loved ones.

In 2019, upon obtaining news of a recurrence, Tiah opted to fight cancer a second time with food and faith. She adopted a whole food plant-based diet, removed products containing toxins or other dangerous chemicals from her home, and added other complementary practices to her daily routine. Tiah believes that making healthy lifestyle changes can help improve one’s quality of life and help with better life outcomes. She is now helping others on their quest for optimal health as a licensed Food for Life Instructor and is educating individuals on ways to eliminate harmful exposures.

Tiah is an activist, an advocate, a Cancer Coach, a Breast Health Educator, a consultant, and a Project Lead graduate of the National Breast Cancer Coalition (NBCC).
